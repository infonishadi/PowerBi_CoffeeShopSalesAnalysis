# PowerBi_CoffeeShopSalesAnalysis
Coffee Shop Sales Analysis Dashboard designed to answer six key business questions. It monitors revenue by location, product category, and transaction hours to support decision-making.

**1.	Which store location has the highest revenue? What might explain this?** 
Hell’s Kitchen has the highest revenue among all three store locations, generating approx. $237K, as shown in the clustered bar chart comparing total revenue by store location.

This higher performance may be due to more customers visiting the store, higher demand for products, or better sales compared to the other locations.

**2.	What are the two peak hours for transactions? How could management use this information?**
Hours 9 and 10 are the two peak periods, as illustrated in the clustered column chart. The highest number of transactions occurs during these hours.

Management can use this insight to allocate maximum staff during peak times to efficiently handle increased sales volume. This helps ensure smooth operations and maintain high customer satisfaction. Additionally, breaks can be scheduled outside these peak hours to avoid understaffing during critical periods.

**3.	Which product category generates the most revenue? Should the company expand this category?**
The Coffee product category generates the highest revenue, contributing 40.83% of total revenue. This is illustrated in the pie chart showing revenue by product category.

The Coffee category shows strong performance and is a good area for expansion, but growth should be controlled and data-driven to avoid over-dependence on a single revenue stream.

**4. Looking at monthly trends, is revenue growing, declining, or stable? What actions might you recommend?**
Based on the Monthly Revenue Trend line chart, revenue is consistently increasing, indicating positive growth.

The company should support this trend by improving staffing, inventory, and logistics to handle higher demand and setting clear sales targets. It is also important to check whether the growth is sustainable or seasonal before making long-term decisions.

**5.	Which individual product is the top seller? What does this tell you about customer preferences?** 
As per the clustered bar chart, the top-selling individual product is Sustainably Grown Organic Lg.

This indicates that customers show a strong preference for health-conscious and sustainable products, suggesting that ethical and organic options are more attractive to customers. This insight can help the business focus more on expanding and promoting sustainable product offerings.

**6.	Looking at the stacked bar chart, does each store have the same product mix, or do some stores sell more of certain categories? What business decisions could this inform?**
Based on the stacked bar chart, all three stores share a similar overall product mix. However, there are differences in category preferences between stores.

For example, Lower Manhattan shows higher sales in bakery items and coffee in their product mix, while Astoria has stronger tea sales, and Loose Tea performs better in Lower Manhattan compared to other locations.
These differences can inform business decisions such as adjusting inventory levels and reorder frequency for specific categories at each store. Additionally, management can use this insight to support staff planning and job rotation, ensuring smooth operations and consistent product availability.
